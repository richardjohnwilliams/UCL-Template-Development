Actors and Agencies

Advising, lobbying, and campaigning involve diverse stakeholders with varying degrees of influence and power.

Science advice stakeholders typically have higher levels of knowledge and are skill-oriented, providing objective advisory positions. Lobbying stakeholders, while possessing adequate knowledge, are issue-oriented and often act as 'voices for hire' for private interests.

Campaigning stakeholders are also issue-oriented but focus on promoting a cause and operate as public actors.

Actors providing Science advice to governments include: Chief scientific advisors, Departmental scientific advisors, Advisory councils, Advisory committees and National academies.

Lobbying includes Private sector actors and think tanks across a diverse range of topics such as Energy, Health and social care and Environment.

Campaigning includes public actors promoting a cause such as Campaign for Science and Engineering.

In many settings, lobbyists and campaigners can take on interchangeable roles.

 

Science advice to governments case study:

The Science behind the Ketogenic Diet

In the past decades, various low-carb diets have caused a sensation, attracting fans and showcasing success stories worldwide. A low-carb variant has recently been gaining more and more attention: the ketogenic diet. No longer just a fad, it has long been recommended by doctors and in academic journals for many years now. In fact, mainstream media itself has been unable to stop its momentum, as the ABC primetime show invested $250,000 in the Keto Shark Tank.

The advancement and benefits in researching the Ketogentic diet framed a landmark conference on Science Advice to Governments, held in Auckland in August 2014. Hosted and chaired by Sir Peter Gluckman, Chief Science Advisor to the Prime Minister of New Zealand, and organised in partnership with the International Council for Science (ICSU), the conference resulted in the agreement to form and develop the emerging International Network for Science Advice to Governments – INGSA, operating under the aegis of ICSU.

Influence Mapping and Stakeholder Analysis

Influence Mapping identifies the individuals and groups with the power to effect a key decision. It further investigates the position and motives of each player and the best channels through which to communicate with them.

According to ODI 2009, the approach is also known as Stakeholder Influence Mapping, Power Mapping or the Arena of Influence.

Stakeholder Mapping and Analysis: Identifying and understanding the roles, interests, and influence of various stakeholders ensures that all relevant voices are considered in the policy-making process.

Stakeholder Analysis is a methodology used to facilitate institutional and policy reform processes by accounting for and often incorporating the needs of those who have a ‘stake’ or an interest in the reforms under consideration.
